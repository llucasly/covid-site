What is COVID-19

Coronavirus more formally known as COVID-19, is from a large family of viruses that cause respiratory infections. COVID-19 is a new virus strain first reported in December 2019 in Wuhan City in China. The virus can spread from person to person with currently no treatment available for it. Source

Symptoms

Most common symptoms:

  • fever
  • dry cough
  • tiredness
  • shortness of breath

Less common symptoms:

  • aches and pains
  • sore throat
  • diarrhoea
  • conjunctivitis
  • headache
  • loss of taste or smell
  • a rash on skin, or discolouration of fingers or toes

Serious symptoms

  • difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
  • chest pain or pressure
  • loss of speech or movement

Prevention

Ways to prevent infection and slow transmission of COVID-19

  • Wash your hands regularly with soap and water, or clean them with alcohol-based hand rub.
  • Maintain at least 1.5 metre distance from others wherever possible.
  • Avoid touching your face.
  • Cover your mouth and nose when coughing or sneezing.
  • Stay home if you feel unwell.
  • Practice physical distancing by avoiding unnecessary travel and staying away from large groups of people.
